Our Offerings

KinPet operates as a multi-sided market. It serves three groups: pet owners, pet care providers, and commercial partners. Each group receives different services, but each group also pays a different trade-off. This reflects broader processes of platformisation, where different services and social activities become organised through shared digital platforms (Poell et al., 2019).

01

For pet owners, KinPet offers three main services. First, it provides health and routine management, including health tracking, vaccination reminders, diet records, and activity logs. Second, it provides service booking, including grooming appointments, training plans, emergency vet access, walking, sitting, and insurance comparisons. Third, it provides community support through local pet groups, advice posts, reviews, and shared pet stories. Instead of moving between separate websites, clinics, shops, and social media groups, owners can manage the whole pet care journey in one place. KinPet promises convenience, peace of mind, and a closer relationship with pets.

02

The trade-off is that this convenience depends on continuous data sharing. Owners give KinPet information about their pets’ breed, age, diet, health, mood, activity, location, spending habits, and service history. This makes recommendations more personal. It also allows the platform to understand when an owner may need a vet, a groomer, a new toy, an insurance plan, or a “premium wellness bundle.”

03

For pet care providers, including vets, trainers, groomers, walkers, and sitters, KinPet also offers three main services. First, it provides appointment tools so providers can manage bookings, availability, and customer messages. Second, it provides customer discovery through search results, local recommendations, and service categories. Third, it provides reputation-building features, including reviews, ratings, profile badges, and paid promotional options. Providers can reach more pet owners without building their own digital infrastructure.

04

The trade-off is that visibility is no longer fully theirs. Providers depend on platform rankings, customer reviews, response speed, and paid promotion. A caring local groomer may become less visible than a sponsored partner with better platform performance. KinPet celebrates small providers as part of the community, but it quietly teaches them that care must also be optimised for the platform.

05

For commercial partners, including pet food brands, insurance companies, product sellers, and wellness services, KinPet offers three main services. First, it provides targeted advertising based on pet profiles, owner interests, and service histories. Second, it provides sponsored recommendations, such as promoted food plans, insurance upgrades, grooming packages, and wellness products. Third, it provides customer insights and campaign placement so brands can understand pet-owner behaviour and reach users at key decision moments. Brands can reach owners when care feels urgent, emotional, or responsible.

The trade-off is that commercial visibility depends on platform participation. Brands must pay for attention, promotion, and recommendation placement. The platform does not simply sell pet products. It connects every caring moment with a possible service, subscription, or purchase.
