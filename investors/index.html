Investor relations

A lean platform for modern pet ownership.

KinPet does not need to own veterinary clinics, grooming shops, training schools, insurance companies, or pet product factories. Its assets are the interface, the Care Engine, user data, service relationships, and the wider ecosystem. (Srnicek, 2017)

Revenue streams 7

Subscriptions, booking commissions, sponsored recommendations, advertising, insurance partnerships, analytics, and loyalty programs.

Core asset Care

Owners contribute profiles, health updates, reviews, photos, service histories, and purchase data.

Growth logic Network

More owners attract more providers and brands; more partners create more options and recommendations.

PF

Platformisation

Separate pet care activities become organised through one ecosystem. Vet care, grooming, walking, training, insurance, shopping, and community engagement can all move through KinPet. (Poell et al., 2019)

IN

Infrastructure positioning

KinPet positions itself not only as a service provider but also as an infrastructure for modern pet ownership. These network effects make the platform harder to replace over time. (Plantin et al., 2018)

AT

Attention economy

KinPet keeps users returning through reminders, alerts, community updates, wellness scores, and personalised care prompts. These tools appear helpful while keeping care inside the platform's daily rhythm. (Webster, 2014)

RO

Risk outsourcing

Providers remain responsible for service quality. Brands remain responsible for product performance. Owners remain responsible for final care decisions. KinPet coordinates the relationships while focusing on data, algorithms, visibility, and ecosystem growth.

Pet care becomes scalable.

Love becomes measurable. Connection becomes a platform business. The more owners love, worry, share, and return, the more KinPet grows.
